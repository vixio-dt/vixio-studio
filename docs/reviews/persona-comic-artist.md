# Vixio Studio — comic pipeline review
Reviewer persona: Rin Okabe, weekly vertical-scroll manga artist. Method: built "Night Courier" (urban fantasy one-shot) end to end against offline preview providers — script → cast → pages → panels → lettering → export → film conversion — plus deliberate break tests. All screenshots in this folder.

## Verdict
Could this replace my thumbnailing + lettering pass for a weekly one-shot? **The thumbnailing half, almost; the lettering half, no.** The script-to-panel-beat pipeline is genuinely better than my current process: I typed a logline, got scenes, planned pages in one click, and every panel arrived pre-loaded with a beat, the right characters, a style-locked prompt, and its own seed. Layout tiers, RTL support, seed lock, take history, and the idempotent film conversion are all real production features, not demos. But the moment I put words on the page it stops being professional tooling: one fixed font at one enormous fixed scale, balloons that amputate their own text when they touch a panel border, no auto-lettering from dialogue the app already knows, and no balloon stacking/joining. The "webtoon strip" export — the format I actually ship — is a page paste-up, not a panel reflow, so it is unusable for vertical-scroll publication. Today I'd use it to break a script into pages and hand the exported JSON to my real tools; I would not letter or publish from it.

## Three things the product gets uniquely right
1. **Script beats flow all the way down.** Scenes auto-distribute into per-panel descriptions with the right characters attached, every panel prompt inlines full character appearance + wardrobe sheets plus comic-style tokens and a "clean art, no speech balloons, no text" guard, and my panel notes append cleanly. One click of "Rebuild prompt" reconciles everything. No comic tool I use connects writing to panel prompts this tightly. (`25-panel-lab.png`, panel-7 prompt dump)
2. **Reproducibility as a first-class feature.** Per-panel seeds with an honest lock toggle ("Every take reuses this seed"), byte-identical takes when locked (verified by hashing), a take strip with seed/renderer/age metadata and one-click restore, and a render queue with live progress. That is exactly the discipline AI comic work needs for revisions. (`33-p7-take-strip-visible.png`, `73-render-queue.png`)
3. **The comic↔film bridge is engineered, not bolted on.** The convert dialog previews per-scene shot diffs (new/updated/unchanged), promises and delivers idempotent re-runs, suggests cameras from panel layout, and carries speech balloons in as speaker-attributed dialogue; afterwards the project flips Film↔Comic losslessly from the header. (`64-convert-dialog.png`, `69-board-scene1.png`, `71-flip-to-comic.png`)
