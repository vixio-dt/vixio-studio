# Vixio Studio — Director's Review ("The Last Ferry", 3-scene two-hander)

Reviewer persona: Lena Voss, short-film director. Method: end-to-end use of the product only (offline preview providers), driven via Playwright at 1440x900. All evidence screenshots live in this directory.

## Verdict

Would I use this for a real animatic/previz pass? **Almost — for early blocking studies yes, for a client-facing animatic not yet.** The skeleton is genuinely right: the pipeline reads like a production board (Script → Cast → Board → Previz → Frames → Motion → Cut), the frame prompts are assembled transparently from my shot grammar and cast sheets, seeds are visible and lockable, and the previz clay captures actually move like the moves I picked — my crane-up rises, my dolly-in advances. That is rarer than it should be in this category. But today the app loses work silently in three different ways (debounced script autosave that drops a scene body, in-flight and even *completed* renders evaporating on reload, generations vanishing on refresh), the final render throws away my shot timings for any shot without a motion clip (my 1:30 timeline rendered as a 0:53 file), there is no undo anywhere, and a two-hander's dialogue cannot be assigned to two voices. A director can't hand a producer a pacing pass from a tool that rewrites the runtime, and can't trust a writing surface that occasionally reverts a scene. Fix the data-loss family and honor durations in the render, and this becomes a tool I'd genuinely block scenes in.

## Three things the product gets uniquely right

1. **A glass-box generation pipeline.** Every frame prompt is assembled *visibly* from shot size/angle/lens/lighting + scene slugline + full character sheets ("Feeds every frame prompt"), is hand-editable with a Rebuild escape hatch, and every take carries provenance ("seed 98895 · Vixio preview renderer · 1m ago"). Seed locking is real: locked seed + regenerate = pixel-identical take. I always knew what the machine was told. (40a, 45b, 41b transcript)
2. **Previz whose camera language is honest.** Preset-seeded A/B keyframes with numeric position/look-at editing, a true through-lens view separate from the blocking orbit view, scrub-interpolation, fast clay captures with a downloadable depth pass — and the moves read correctly in the captured clips: crane-up climbs from low-close to overhead, dolly-in closes from full figure to chest-up. (33c, 34-clip-t0/t3/t5_8, 36-dolly-t0/t5_8, 37b)
3. **A pipeline that degrades gracefully instead of blocking.** Missing work becomes visible state, not errors: warning triangles on unready shots, "6/17 clips rendered" in the Cut header, un-rendered shots becoming labeled slate cards with burned captions in the final file, one-click contact-sheet PNG and cut-data JSON exports for handoff. The film always plays end to end. (46-motion-initial, 50-cut-initial, 61-final-t10/t21, 65a)
